## 1. Classic Black

**Website:** [classicblackmusic.org](https://www.classicblackmusic.org/)
**Arterial page:** [arterial.org/classic-black](https://arterial.org/classic-black/)
**Relationship to Arterial:** Fiscal sponsorship (Arterial is the 501(c)(3) backbone)

### What It Is

Classic Black is an educational platform and performance program celebrating Black excellence in classical music, from historical composers to contemporary performers. Founded by Steinway Artist Ric'key Pageot in partnership with Crewest Studio, the program combines live musical performances with educational presentations designed for schools, organizations, and community venues.

### Mission

To honor and celebrate the contributions of Black composers to classical music — figures who have been historically underrepresented in the classical canon. The program addresses the frustration that classical musicians of color often discover composers like William Grant Still, Joseph Bologne, and Florence B. Price much later than European counterparts like Bach and Mozart.

### Featured Composers

The program spotlights historically significant Black composers including:
- **Joseph Boulogne** (Chevalier de Saint-Georges) — 18th-century violinist and composer
- **George Bridgetower** — violinist who premiered Beethoven's "Kreutzer Sonata"
- **Thomas "Blind Tom" Wiggins** — 19th-century piano prodigy
- **Samuel Coleridge-Taylor** — British composer of Sierra Leonean descent
- **Florence B. Price** — first African-American woman to have a composition played by a major U.S. orchestra
- **William Grant Still** — "Dean of African-American Composers"
- **George Walker** — first African-American to win a Pulitzer Prize in Music
- **Howard Swanson** — mid-20th century American composer

### Who Is Ric'key Pageot

**Background:**
- Montreal-born pianist from a Haitian family
- Studied at Pierre-Laporte School's professional arts program (classical piano, music theory, history, ear training)
- Further studies at McGill University
- Early career: performed with the Montreal Symphony Orchestra under conductor Charles Dutoit

**Career Highlights:**
- **Steinway Artist** — officially designated; Madonna personally sent him to Steinway's factory to select a grand piano for the Madame X tour
- **Madonna's touring pianist** for over a decade, performing on four world tours:
  - Sticky & Sweet (2008-2009)
  - MDNA
  - Rebel Heart
  - Madame X
- **Christina Aguilera** — touring pianist
- **Cirque du Soleil** — youngest music director ever appointed, for "Delirium" production (~200 U.S. shows, 2005)
- **Corneille** — toured France (platinum-certified album)
- **Motown Generation** — original cast member (2002), Canada's most successful Motown revue
- **Notable collaborations:** Earth Wind & Fire, Jill Scott, Diana Ross, Cher, Dr. John

**Other Projects:**
- **Parlor Social** — band with wife Dessy Di Lauro, creating "Speakeasy-soul/Prohibition Funk" blending 1920s-30s jazz with contemporary urban sounds. 2013 debut album received critical acclaim. Performed at Montreal Jazz Festival and Hollywood Bowl.

## 2. The Underbelly Project

**Website:** [theunderbellyproject.com](https://theunderbellyproject.com/)

**Relationship to Arterial:** Grant recipient ($12,000)

### The Original Art Installation (2009-2010)

The Underbelly Project was one of the most significant underground art events in modern history. From early 2009 to mid-2010, NYC-based curators **Workhorse** and **PAC** secretly transformed an abandoned New York City subway station into the world's largest underground art gallery.

**Key facts about the installation:**
- **Location:** An abandoned NYC subway station (exact location kept secret)
- **Duration:** Early 2009 to mid-2010
- **Artists:** Over 100 street artists participated
- **Process:** Each artist worked alone for one night, using only flashlights to illuminate the station's walls, pillars, and platforms
- **Secrecy:** The project was kept completely secret during its creation
- **Current status:** The MTA boarded up the space, making it a time capsule for future generations

**Notable Participating Artists:**
- Ron English
- Swoon
- Faile
- Revok
- Shepard Fairey
- Logan Hicks

## 3. The Good Art

**Relationship to Arterial:** In development via Crewest Studio (Arterial's production partner)

### What It Is

"The Good Art" is an **animated documentary about the lowbrow art movement**, currently in development at Crewest Studio. Limited public information is available about this project.

### The Lowbrow Art Movement (Subject Matter)

The lowbrow art movement (also called Pop Surrealism) is an underground visual art movement that arose in the Los Angeles area in the late 1960s-1970s, with cultural roots in:
- Underground comix (R. Crumb, etc.)
- Punk music culture
- Tiki culture
- Graffiti and street art
- Hot-rod and custom car culture
- Skateboard graphics
- Psychedelic art

Key figures include Robert Williams (who coined "Lowbrow"), Mark Ryden, Gary Baseman, Todd Schorr, and many others. The movement has strong connections to the LA gallery scene, particularly Juxtapoz Magazine (founded by Robert Williams) and galleries like Copro Gallery, Merry Karnowsky Gallery, and others.

## 5. Additional Sub-Projects

### 5.1 Artsville USA

**Website:** [artsvilleusa.com](https://www.artsvilleusa.com/)
**Relationship to Arterial:** Acquired by Arterial

**What It Is:**
ArtsvilleUSA is a digital platform celebrating American contemporary arts and crafts, focused on the Asheville, Western North Carolina arts community. Describes itself as "a group of artists, art professionals + enthusiasts supporting the arts community in Asheville."

**Founders / Leadership:**
- **Louise Glickman** — founder, based in Asheville
- **Daryl Slaton** — co-founder, artist
- **Scott Power** — LA-based partner via Crewest Studio; Artsville producer

**Content & Programs:**
- **Artsville Podcast** — arts-focused podcast covering regional and national art topics; at least 27+ episodes documented (episode 27 and 28 referenced in search results)
- **Feature articles** — stories on local artists and cultural events
- **Exhibition coverage** — including winter exhibition "Liminal Light: Winter's Vigil"
- **Open calls** for artists (exhibitions, holiday markets)
- **Newsletter** — monthly cultural roundups
- **Virtual gallery** — launched March 2024
- **Helene Stories Hotline** — launched after Hurricane Helene to help artists document experiences

**Content Focus:**
World-class potters, weavers, glassblowers, wood workers, painters, and other craft artists from Asheville and the broader Western NC region.

**Hurricane Helene Impact (2024):**
The Asheville arts community was devastated by Hurricane Helene. Artsville documented the impact and advocated for support and funding to sustain artists' crafts in the aftermath.

### 5.2 Arthouse (TV Series)

**Websites:**
- [creweststudio.com/arthouse-tv-series](https://creweststudio.com/arthouse-tv-series/)
- [Indiegogo campaign](https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/arthouse-tv-show)
**Relationship to Arterial:** Fiscal sponsorship (donations are tax-deductible via Arterial's 501(c)(3) status)

**What It Is:**
Arthouse is an unscripted television series designed to democratize art discovery and purchasing for everyday Americans. The show follows individuals and families with budgets ranging from $100 to $10,000 as they discover original art from local artists.

**Format:**
Each episode features genuine art buyers visiting local artists' studios, learning creative processes, and discovering pieces that resonate personally. Described as "no pretension — just real people falling in love with art."

**Creative Team:**

- **Laura Patterson** — Director (Directors Guild member; directed multiple seasons of HGTV's "House Hunters")
- **Carmen Acosta** — Host (contemporary artist, designer, and curator from Los Angeles)
