## 1. Arterial.org — Organization Overview

### What Is Arterial?

Arterial is a **501(c)(3) non-profit media organization** based in **Los Angeles**, with operations in **New York City** and **Asheville, North Carolina**. It was **founded in 2023** by Scott "Sourdough" Power as **Arterial, Inc.**

Arterial describes itself as a "modern entertainment media platform dedicated exclusively to amplifying human creativity."

**Website:** [arterial.org](https://arterial.org/)
**Tagline:** "Amplify Art"

### Mission

Arterial's mission is to make the creative arts more accessible through programs that **entertain, inform, and inspire** people. The organization's stated intent is to:

- **Demystify the art world** — make art accessible to general audiences, not just insiders
- **Humanize artists** — tell the personal stories behind the work
- **Amplify art** — surface important stories that would likely go untold by traditional media companies due to commercial concerns

Arterial's goal is "enduring positive social impact for the commonwealth rather than financial profit."

### How It Works

Arterial operates across multiple media verticals:

- **Film** — documentary production and distribution
- **Television** — unscripted TV series development (Arthouse)
- **Podcasting** — the NOT REAL ART Podcast Network (6+ podcasts, 300+ hours)
- **Radio** — Arterial Radio (internet-based talk radio dedicated to creative culture, in development)
- **Publishing** — blog content, artist profiles, exhibitions coverage
- **Events & Activations** — live events, gallery exhibitions, art fairs

### Core Programs & Projects

## 2. Scott "Sourdough" Power — Founder & CEO

### Biography

Scott "Sourdough" Power is the **founder and CEO of Arterial, Inc.** He has worked professionally in the creative arts **since 1988** (age 18). He is a **multi-disciplinary artist, producer, curator, and consultant**.

**Origin:** Born in a steel town near Chicago, Illinois. Blue-collar Midwestern roots. Grandson of a minister; mother was a church choir director; father a Sunday school teacher. Raised in a musical family, Scott studied jazz and classical music, played alto saxophone, and won multiple competitions.

**The "Sourdough" nickname:** Earned while living in the **Canadian Arctic**, where he perfected his sourdough pancakes. He hosts the NOT REAL ART podcast under this pseudonym.

### Career Highlights

- **30+ years** in the creative and visual arts
- Held key strategic and management positions across visual arts, publishing, and advertising
- Curated private and public art exhibitions worldwide
- Produced live events and helped organizations integrate creative arts into their programming

## 4. Crewest Studio

### Overview

**Crewest Studio** is an independent arts media and entertainment company based in **Los Angeles**. It is co-founded by **Scott "Sourdough" Power** and **Alex Poli (aka Man One)**.

**Website:** [creweststudio.com](https://creweststudio.com/)

### History

**Crewest Gallery (2002–2012/2014):**
- Founded in **2002** by **Man One (Alex Poli)** and business partner **Harry Reynolds**
- Was the **first gallery in Los Angeles** — and one of the first in the world — to **legitimize graffiti as a contemporary art form**
- Initially located in **Alhambra, California**
- Relocated in **2006** to **Gallery Row in Downtown Los Angeles** (110 Winston Street)
- Over 10 years, produced **100+ art exhibitions** featuring **2,000+ artists** from **20+ countries**
- Featured major artists: Chaz Bojorquez, Cope2, Daim, Blek Le Rat, Logan Hicks, Os Gemeos, Retna, Mr. Cartoon, Saber, Shepard Fairey (invited), DabsMyla, Patrick Martinez, Hex, Craola, Overton Loyd, Syd Mead, and many more
- Declared **"mission accomplished"** and closed, transitioning to Crewest Studio
- [PBS SoCal feature on Crewest Gallery closing](https://www.pbssocal.org/history-society/crewest-gallery-moving-on-after-a-mission-accomplished)

**Crewest Studio (current):**
- Operates as an **independent media and entertainment company** focused on contemporary art and culture
- Produces **podcasts, documentaries, events, and publishing** content
- Described as a "progressive brand consultancy" that helps organizations "imagine, create, and produce fresh ideas"
- Serves as a **strategic partner** of Arterial

### Alex Poli / Man One

- Born **Alejandro Poli Jr., 1971**, in **East Los Angeles**
- **Mexican-American mural and graffiti artist**
- Best known for **popularizing West Coast graffiti** to an international audience
- Graduated from **Loyola Marymount University** with a degree in art
- **LinkedIn:** [linkedin.com/in/alexpoli1](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexpoli1/)
- [PBS SoCal Artbound episode: "Man One With One Man's Goal: Make Graffiti Legit"](https://www.pbssocal.org/shows/artbound/man-one-with-one-mans-goal-make-graffiti-legit)

### Relationship with Arterial

Crewest Studio and Arterial have a **deep strategic partnership**. Crewest Studio's website hosts Arterial announcements, and several Arterial programs (Classic Black, Arthouse, podcast content) are co-produced with or channeled through Crewest Studio. Scott Power co-founded both entities.

### Sub-Programs

- **Host:** Scott "Sourdough" Power
- In-depth conversations with contemporary artists and creative professionals
- Part of a broader **podcast network** with multiple shows
- Available on Apple Podcasts, Spotify, iHeart, TuneIn, SoundCloud, and more

#### NOT REAL ART Podcast Network
- Includes multiple podcasts under the Crewest Studio / NOT REAL ART umbrella:
  - **NOT REAL ART** — flagship arts interview podcast

  - **Artsville** — American arts & crafts from Asheville and beyond

## 8. Arthouse — TV Series

### Overview

Arthouse is an **unscripted TV series** about how Americans discover and purchase art. It is an **Arterial-sponsored project** currently in **pilot production**.

### Format

- Viewers meet **real art buyers** with authentic stories about why they want to bring art into their homes
- Guided by host, they visit **local artists in their studios**
- Learn about different creative processes and discover pieces that speak to them personally
- Each episode features buyers with **budgets ranging from $100 to $10,000**
- Core message: "original art isn't reserved for millionaires or Manhattan penthouses"

### Key Team

| Role | Person | Background |
|------|--------|------------|
| Creator & Executive Producer | **Scott "Sourdough" Power** | Working in visual/performing arts since 1988 |
| Host | **Carmen Acosta** | Contemporary artist, designer, and curator from Los Angeles |

## 9. The Underbelly Project — Documentary

### The Original Art Installation (2009–2010)

- NYC-based curators **Workhorse** and **PAC** transformed an **abandoned New York City subway station** in South Williamsburg into a secret art installation
- **103 street artists** participated, each working alone for one night using only flashlights
- Notable invited artists: **Ron English, Swoon, Faile, Revok, Shepard Fairey, Logan Hicks**
- **Banksy** was invited and "loved the concept" but declined to work on his own film
- Publicly revealed on **Halloween 2010** via *The New York Times* and other major outlets
- The installation is **permanently sealed** — inaccessible to the public

### The Documentary Film

- **Directed by:** Workhorse and PAC (the original curators)
- Contains **extensive footage** from the original project, artist interviews, and creation process
- Film remained **unfinished for years** due to legal and funding constraints
- **Arterial funded the completion** of post-production through a grant
- **IMDb:** [The Underbelly Project (2023)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9364392/)
- **Premiered:** December 1, 2024, at **Miami Art Week** (Wynwood Walls)
- 200 attendees at the premiere including art critics, collectors, and original participating artists
- Post-screening panel discussion with Workhorse and PAC

Source: [Arterial grant announcement](https://arterial.org/underbelly-project-documentary/)

## 10. Classic Black — Music Program

### Overview

**Classic Black** celebrates **Black excellence in classical music**. The program was created by **Ric'key Pageot** — a Montreal-born pianist, accordionist, producer, composer, and music director who is also a **Steinway Artist**.

### Partnership Structure

- Created by **Ric'key Pageot** in collaboration with **Crewest Studio**
- Received **fiscal sponsorship from Arterial** — launched two media series in 2024

### Media

- **"Classic Black: The Video"** — documents Ric'key Pageot's performances and personal narrative
- **"Classic Black: The Podcast"** — discussions about Black composers' contributions to classical music; connects historical works to contemporary classical music education

### Featured Composers

The program highlights great Black composers including:
- Joseph Boulogne (Chevalier de St. Georges)
- George Bridgetower
- Thomas "Blind Tom" Wiggins
- Samuel Coleridge-Taylor
- Florence B. Price
- William Grant Still
- George Walker

**Website:** [classicblackmusic.org](https://www.classicblackmusic.org/)

Source: [Arterial Celebrates Black Excellence in Classical Music](https://arterial.org/classic-black/)
