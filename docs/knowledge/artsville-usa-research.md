## 1. What Is Artsville USA?

### Mission & Identity

Artsville USA (artsvilleusa.com) is a digital platform and arts organization celebrating contemporary American arts and crafts, with a primary focus on Western North Carolina (WNC) and Asheville. It operates as **a wholly owned subsidiary of Arterial, Inc.**, Scott Power's Los Angeles-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Despite being structured as a for-profit company, Artsville functions as a "disregarded entity" for tax purposes, making donations designated for Artsville fully tax-deductible.

The platform's stated mission is to bring "opportunities and benefits to artists and art lovers in Asheville and beyond," welcoming participants globally to engage with exhibitions, podcasts, blogs, virtual and live events, and discussion groups.

### Founding Story

Artsville emerged from the **Sand Hill Artists Collective (SHAC)**, which began in late 2019 when **Louise Glickman**, facing vision deterioration, sought to stay active in the art community. She and her husband, artist **Daryl Slaton**, organized an interest group among Biltmore Lake artists (the couple has lived in the Biltmore Lake community since 2006). The group expanded across western Buncombe County, then to all of Western North Carolina by 2022.

Artsville was co-founded by:
- **Louise Glickman** — Founder and Director (business background, organizational vision)
- **Daryl Slaton** — Co-founder and artist (Glickman's husband)
- **Scott "Sourdough" Power** — Co-founder, Crewest Studio/LA partner (podcast production, national/international reach)

The partnership with **Crewest Studio/LA** (Scott Power's Los Angeles-based creative studio) brought professional podcast production, distribution expertise, and a three-year track record of international arts media reach.

## 2. What Artsville USA Does — Core Programs

### Virtual Gallery of Artists (VGA)

The centerpiece of the platform. A zero-commission online gallery where artists showcase work through artist statements, headshots, and images of their art. All sales are conducted directly between buyers and artists — Artsville takes no commission.

- **First cohort launched:** October 15, 2023 with 9 artists
- **Expanded in March 2024** from 9 to 15 artists after record applicant numbers
- Artists receive 3 complimentary months of marketing and mentoring
- Mediums span ceramics, jewelry, oil painting, photography, printmaking, hot wax artistry, and more

**First Cohort Artists (2023):** Amy Massey, Candice Hensley, Elizabeth Walton, Georgia Deal, Jean McLaughlin, Joseph Pearson, Max Cooper, Selene Plum, Wendy Newman

**Second Cohort Artists (2024):** Amanda Cook, Anne Asplin, Jodie Karr, Elle Tracey, Emelie Weber Wade, Sarah Garrard, Gail Powell, Isabelle Klauder, Justin Breitzke, Kurt Ross, Misty Kelischek, Cynthia Llanes, Rebecca Horne, Sue Dolamore, Tiffany Payne

### The Artsville Podcast

A professionally produced podcast celebrating contemporary American arts and crafts, co-produced with Crewest Studio/LA. Recognized as one of the **Top 10 crafts-focused podcasts in the country** (January 2024).

- Available on Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Google, Amazon Music, and via Captivate.fm
- Features interviews with WNC artists, makers, gallery directors, arts organizations, and community leaders
- 30+ episodes produced to date
- New host **Elise Wilson** took over in 2025, with plans for revamped production quality in 2026

**Notable podcast guests include:**
- Arthur Roger (Arthur Roger Gallery, New Orleans)
- Michael Manes (Blue Spiral 1 gallery, Asheville)
- Candace Reilly (North Carolina Glass Center)
- Sue Dolamore (watercolor artist and educator)
- Jamie Karolich (Craft Your Commerce)
- Marla Tambellini (Explore Asheville SVP Marketing)
- Andy Palmer and Vicki Essig (art and community in Bakersville, NC)
- Sarah Benoit (artist marketing)
- Penland School of Craft representatives

### Q+ART Interview Series

Launched April 2024 in partnership with **NOT REAL ART** (Scott Power's other Arterial project). A written interview series highlighting Asheville's creative community, published on artsvilleusa.com.

### The Artsville Connection (Blog & Newsletter)

Bi-weekly blogs and monthly newsletters covering art, cultural travel, personal commentary, artist features, and WNC arts community news. Topics include:
- Artist profiles and studio visits
- Photo essays
- Exhibition previews and reviews
- Community event roundups
- Travelogues (e.g., American Craft Made Baltimore)
- Arts industry commentary

### Physical Gallery Space

**Artsville Collective at Marquee** — located at 36 Foundy Street in Asheville's River Arts District. A joint partnership of Sand Hill Artists Collective and Crewest Studios/LA, offering rotating exhibitions for emerging artists.

Inaugural show: *"Between the Lines"* featuring artist Micah Mackenzie and ceramicist Rhona Polonsky (through April 24, 2022).

### Artist Residency Program (launched 2025)

Artsville's inaugural residency sent Asheville artist **Kenn Kotara** to **Mammoth Lakes, California** in partnership with the **Mono Arts Council**. During the residency, Kotara taught cartography to children, led frottage printing workshops, and created work inspired by alpine landscapes and sequoia groves.

### Craft Your Commerce

Business education workshops for artists, featured in podcast episodes and newsletter content.

## 3. New Orleans Gallery Partnerships & Joint Exhibitions

### "A Tale of Two Cities" (ATOTC) — The Major Exhibition

This is the signature cross-regional exhibition connecting New Orleans and Asheville through shared hurricane recovery narratives.

**Exhibition Details:**
- **Dates:** September 13 – November 8, 2025
- **Physical Location:** New Orleans Academy of Fine Arts (NOAFA)
- **Virtual Exhibition:** Launched September 1, 2025 on ArtsvilleUSA.com (viewable through end of 2025)
- **Asheville Satellite:** Ferguson Family YMCA in Candler, North Carolina
- **Opening Reception:** September 13, 2025 at 8 p.m. at NOAFA

**Strategic Timing:** The opening date of September 13, 2025 was deliberately positioned between the 20th anniversary of Hurricane Katrina (August 29, 2005) and the first anniversary of Hurricane Helene (September 27, 2024).

**Partner Organizations:**
1. **New Orleans Academy of Fine Arts (NOAFA)** — host venue
2. **ArtsvilleUSA** — co-producer, virtual exhibition host
3. **River Arts District Artists (RADA)** and **RADA Foundation** — Asheville artist organization
4. **Blue Ridge National Heritage Area (BRNHA)** — heritage area sponsor

**Curators:**
- Jan Gilbert (New Orleans artist)
- Andrew J. Rodgers (NOAFA Executive Director)
- Kenn Kotara (Asheville-based artist)
- Kim Self Hundertmark (RADA Foundation Executive Director)

**Exhibiting Artists (40+ total):**

*New Orleans (12 artists):*
Luis Cruz Azaceta, Babette Beaullieu, Keith Calhoun, Tina Freeman, Jan Gilbert, Jeremy Jernegan, Chandra McCormick, Jana Napoli, Sibylle Peretti, Rontherin Ratliff, Frank Relle, Phil Sandusky

*Asheville/WNC (20 artists):*
Rob Amberg, Tami Beldue, Galen Frost Bernard, Jeffrey Burroughs, Cassie Butcher, Elizabeth Carrington, Alison Chism, Ashley Graber, Danielle Hughes, Jesse Jason, Chris Jehly, Kenn Kotara, Nava Lubelski, Melanie Maranda, Jim McDowell, Joseph Pearson, Amy Putansu, Peter Roux, Julie Slattery, Hayden Wilson, Laura Woods

*Blue Ridge Craft Trails Region (10+ exhibitors):*
Bandana Pottery, Pam Brewer, Crossnore Weavers, Rachel David, John Geci, Bob Kogut, Rodney Leftwich, Mike McKinney, William Rogers, Desmond Suarez, Mary Thompson

**Notable Artworks:**
- *"Louisiana Prayer Flags"* by Jan Gilbert and Babette Beaullieu — meditative installation merging Tibetan, Japanese, and Cajun traditions
- *"overtopping"* by Kenn Kotara — depicts a breached levee with cascading water across a gridded city
- *"Clouet"* by Frank Relle
- *"Elysian Field"* by Sibylle Peretti
- *"Overreach"* by Rachel David
- *"Things That Float"* by Rontherin Ratliff
- *"Moment by Moment"* by Peter Roux
- *"Community"* by Rob Amberg

**Financial Model:** All proceeds from artwork sales went directly to participating Western North Carolina artists to support regional Hurricane Helene recovery. The artwork traveled 650 miles from Asheville to New Orleans.

**Supporting Events:**
- **Speaker Series:** September 12 (5–7 p.m.) and September 13 (4–5:30 p.m.) featuring regional artists
- **Artist Panel (live stream):** September 14 at RAD Welcome Center
- **RAD Resilience:** Community art event supporting Helene-displaced artists (September 13)
- **RADfest:** Second annual event coinciding with exhibition closing (November 8)

**Key Quotes:**

> "There is shared trauma between our cities... This exhibition was a small way the Academy could help Asheville in its journey of healing." — Andrew Rodgers, NOAFA Executive Director

> "We aimed to build a network while recognizing that this is a widespread issue... recovery takes a long time and continues." — Jan Gilbert

> "Moving from the loner artist... to more community-minded, because it's so needed." — Kenn Kotara

> "Art is the highest form of hope." — Jan Gilbert (paraphrasing Gerhard Richter)

### New Orleans Galleries Referenced

1. **New Orleans Academy of Fine Arts (NOAFA)** — Primary exhibition venue for ATOTC; Executive Director Andrew J. Rodgers served as co-curator
2. **Arthur Roger Gallery** — Founded 1978 in the New Orleans Arts District by Arthur Roger (native of New Orleans); 45-year history; spearheaded post-Katrina visual arts renewal; Arthur Roger was a guest on the Artsville podcast
3. **Blue Spiral 1** (Asheville counterpart) — Three-story Asheville gallery founded January 1, 1990 by John Cram; gallery director Michael Manes appeared on the Artsville podcast alongside Arthur Roger to discuss cross-city hurricane recovery lessons

### Podcast Episode: "A Tale of Two Cities: What Asheville Can Learn From New Orleans"

A dedicated podcast episode featured **Michael Manes** (Blue Spiral 1, Asheville) and **Arthur Roger** (Arthur Roger Gallery, New Orleans) discussing how the New Orleans arts community rebuilt after Hurricane Katrina, with lessons for Asheville post-Helene.

## 6. Recent Activity (2024–2026)

### 2024 Highlights
- Virtual Gallery expanded from 9 to 15 artists (March 2024)
- Q+ART interview series launched in partnership with NOT REAL ART (April 2024)
- Podcast recognized as Top 10 crafts podcast in the country (January 2024)
- Joseph Pearson (VGA alumnus) honored for public mural in Asheville's Black Southside neighborhood
- **Hurricane Helene** devastated WNC arts community (September 27, 2024)
- Year-end podcast episode with Scott Power and Louise Glickman addressing Helene's impact
- Helene Stories Hotline launched for artists to share experiences

### 2025 Highlights
- **"A Tale of Two Cities"** exhibition at NOAFA, New Orleans (September 13 – November 8, 2025) — the signature achievement
- Inaugural **artist residency** (Kenn Kotara in Mammoth Lakes, CA)
- Elise Wilson became Executive Director and new podcast host
- "Reawakening" exhibition for National Craft Month
- Blue Ridge Craft Trails Invitational online exhibition
- "Hurricane Helene: From the Beginning" photo exhibition
- "Liminal Light: Winter's Vigil" online exhibition
- Live Holiday Market at YMCA
- Coverage of American Craft Made Baltimore
- Featured stories on Afghan Women's Craft Collective, Street Creature Puppet Collective, LEAF Global Arts Festival, Noir Collective AVL

### 2026 Plans (Announced)
- **Louise Glickman Grant & Residency** — $10,000 investment supporting a craft artist plus companion for a two-week August residency (covers travel, housing, per diem). Named after ArtsvilleUSA's founder.
- **PAVE Program** — "Partner Archival Virtual Exhibitions" creating permanent digital archives of temporary physical exhibitions to extend artist reach and sales
- **Revamped Podcast** — Increased production quality with new host Elise Wilson and expanded guest caliber
- **Membership Program** — First official membership drive launched February 2026, with custom textile prizes from Asheville artists
