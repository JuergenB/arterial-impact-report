## 1. What Is Arthouse?

**Arthouse** is an unscripted (reality) television series created by Scott "Sourdough" Power that transforms how everyday Americans discover and purchase original art. The format is similar to HGTV's *House Hunters*, but instead of houses, buyers are shopping for original artwork.

### Format

Each 30-minute episode follows an individual, couple, or family as they:
1. Are introduced with their story about why they want to bring original art into their home
2. Visit **3 local artists** in their studios to learn about their creative processes
3. See each artist's work and understand different styles and price points
4. Choose the artist whose work resonates most with them
5. Experience a **dramatic reveal** of the selected artwork installed in their home

Budget ranges featured span from **$100 to $10,000**, making art accessible across income levels.

### Mission

The series aims to:
- Demystify the art world and eliminate the intimidation factor of art collecting
- Connect everyday people directly with working artists (bypassing galleries)
- Create direct economic impact for local artists
- Showcase diverse artistic styles and communities across the country
- Prove to networks that art-focused programming has mainstream appeal

1. **Theresa Hubbard (CEO)** appeared on the NOT REAL ART podcast to discuss the Arthouse project and the importance of fiscal sponsorship for creative projects. She stated: *"One of the things that really inspired me about [Arthouse] was this idea of bringing art buyers and art patrons into the community with artists and helping them foster relationships."*

2. **Nathan Zebedeo**, Director of Fractured Atlas's Programs team (responsible for fiscal sponsorship applications), was personally thanked by artist Kim Knoll for his involvement in the Arthouse pilot filming — suggesting he played a hands-on role in the production.

3. **NOT REAL ART fundraiser article** explicitly lists Fractured Atlas as providing "additional support" alongside Arterial's fiscal sponsorship.

### Important Clarification: Fiscal Sponsor

**Arterial** (Scott Power's own 501(c)(3)) is the **fiscal sponsor** for the Arthouse Indiegogo campaign — NOT Fractured Atlas. Donations go through Arterial, and Arterial issues the tax-deductible receipts. Fractured Atlas is a **partner/supporter** of the project, not the fiscal sponsor.

## 7. Distribution Strategy

### Current Plan

The Arthouse distribution strategy operates on **two tracks**:

**Track 1: Network/Streaming Sale (Primary Goal)**
- The pilot episode serves as a pitch tool to sell the series to a major network or streaming platform
- Target platforms explicitly mentioned: **Netflix, Hulu, or Apple TV**
- The crowdfunding campaign functions as "proof of demand" — demonstrating audience interest before approaching distributors
- The experienced production team (HGTV, Netflix, MTV credits) brings industry relationships

**Track 2: Random Media Distribution (Fallback/Complement)**
- Arterial's partnership with Random Media ensures the content can reach streaming audiences independently
- This provides a distribution path even if a major network deal doesn't materialize
- Random Media specializes in independent content distribution to streaming platforms

### Strategic Positioning

The Arthouse team positions the show as:
- A gap in the market — no existing show does for art what *House Hunters* does for real estate
- Mainstream-friendly format (familiar reality TV structure) with a culturally enriching twist
- Community-driven (crowdfunding backers become evangelists)
- Scalable to any city/region in the country (each episode features different local artists)
