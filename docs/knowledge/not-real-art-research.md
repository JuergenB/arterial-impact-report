## 1. Organization Overview

**NOT REAL ART** is a platform dedicated to contemporary arts, culture, and design, operating under the tagline **"Where Artists Rule."** It was created by **Scott "Sourdough" Power** to help amplify and share artists' talents with the world.

The name is intentionally satirical — it parodies gatekeepers who question the legitimacy of artists and their art ("Are they a Real Artist? Do they make Real Art?"). The organization describes itself as **"art cheerleaders, not art critics,"** recognizing that artists have abundant critics but need more supporters.

NOT REAL ART positions itself as **"a progressive alternative to art world conservatism, a place where art is for all and all are welcome."**

**Organizational structure:** NOT REAL ART is a production of **Crewest Studio**, based in Los Angeles, CA (8560 W. Sunset Blvd. S. 500, Los Angeles, CA 90069). It is powered by **Arterial, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit media organization**, making donations tax-deductible.

**Website:** notrealart.com
**Social Media:** Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter/X, SoundCloud

## 3. NOT REAL ART Grant Program

### Key Stats
- **Founded:** 2018 (by Scott "Sourdough" Power and **Channing Dungey Power**, then Chairman of Warner Brothers Television, now VP at Netflix)
- **Partner:** Art Share LA (a 501(c)(3) nonprofit in the Los Angeles Arts District providing 30 affordable live/work lofts for artists)
- **Annual award total:** $12,000
- **Per artist:** $2,000 (no strings attached)
- **Recipients per cycle:** 6 artists (originally 12 in 2019)
- **Schedule:** Originally annual; changed to **biennial** (every two years) after 2022
- **Total grant recipients to date:** At least 36 artists across 5 cycles (2019-2024)

### Grant History by Year

#### 2019 (Inaugural) — 12 recipients, $1,000 each
Elizabeth Abaravich, Edmund Arevalo, Maria Balseiro, Juliana Bustillo, Talya Coviello, Monica Leal Cueva, Eben Eldridge, Marguerite Kalhor, Karen Florito, Thony Loui (traveled from Haiti), Jessie Noguchi, Rachel O'Donnell

- Announced at the NOT REAL ART Creators Conference, March 16, 2019
- Exhibition held at **Art Share LA**, October 19, 2019
- Over 200 attendees; Hans Fjellestad performed; show books distributed

#### 2020 — 6 recipients, $2,000 each
Gershon Kreimer, Kiara Aileen Machado, Miki Yokoyama, Paloma Montoya, Jacqueline Valenzuela, Tijera Williams

- Exhibition in partnership with **Culver City Arts, Helms Bakery District, and Projecting Possibilities** (October 9-15)

#### 2021 — 6 recipients, $2,000 each
John Chang, Nadyia Duff, Daniela Garcia, Carmen Mardonez, Natalia Villanueva, Albert Willis

- Exhibition at **Helms Bakery District** in partnership with **Culver City Arts Foundation** (August 13-19)
- Judges: MonaLisa Whitaker, Man One, Heidi Johnson, Cheyanne Sauter, Scott "Sourdough" Power

#### 2022 — 6 recipients, $2,000 each
Kiley Ames, Buena Johnson, Ellamaria Foley-Ray, Y Hope Osborn, Jo-Ann Morgan, Joan Cox

- Selected from over 300 applicants

#### 2024 — 6 recipients, $2,000 each (biennial cycle)
Alison Hiltner, Casey Fletcher, Danielle O'Malley, Jessie Rodriguez, Jordan Vinyard, Nastassja Swift

- Selected from over **1,200 applicants** (largest pool in grant history)
- Judges: Man One, Morgan Laurens, Hans Fjellestad, Badir McCleary, Anne Martin, Jonmarc Endwards, Joshua Wattles
- Work included stop-motion animation, kinetic sculpture, and needle-felted portraits

### Application Process
- Artists submit contact details, biography, artist statement, and up to 5 artwork samples online
- Judged on four metrics: **technical merit, artistic merit, conceptual merit, and written merit**
- Focus on artists demonstrating "strong sense of ingenuity, emotion and craft"
- Targets "impactful ideas from marginalized communities, underrepresented voices, and work that operates outside the standard gallery or museum system"

### Beyond the Cash
All grant recipients receive:
- Featured interview on the NOT REAL ART Podcast
- Full-length feature article published on NOT REAL ART
- Showcase in an annual gallery exhibition
- Past recipients have gone on to receive private commissions, book features, gallery exhibitions, and additional grants through the extended NOT REAL ART community network

### Key Quote
Scott Power: *"The money is great, but the grant is also about audience and promotion."*

## 5. First Friday Exhibitions

### Overview
First Friday Exhibitions is a **curated monthly online exhibition series** launched in **2023**. A new show opens on the first Friday of every month, featuring contemporary artists organized around a specific theme.

Each exhibition is hosted on the NOT REAL ART website using **Artwork Archive's** embedded viewer for digital artwork display. The series has featured work from **hundreds of contemporary artists** across all editions.

### Total Exhibition Count
- **2023:** At least 10 exhibitions (the 10th, "Down a Long, Dark Corridor," was published November 3, 2023)
- **2024:** At least 12 exhibitions (monthly schedule)
- **2025:** 12 exhibitions scheduled (full year announced)
- **Running total: 30+ exhibitions** since launch

## 7. Remote Video Series

### Overview
**Remote** is a video documentary series exploring **public art across the United States**, created by L.A. filmmaker **Badir McCleary** in partnership with NOT REAL ART. The series is exclusively available on the NOT REAL ART platform.

### Season 1 (Released March 2024)
**6 episodes:**

1. **"Desert X"** — Coachella Valley, California (Desert X installations)
2. **"Aesthetic Information as Public Art"** — Philadelphia, Pennsylvania (murals, art renaissance)
3. **"Perception of Public Art"** — Los Angeles, California (Michael Heizer's *Levitated Mass*, galleries)
4. **"Becoming a Public Art City"** — San Antonio, Texas (cultural renaissance, public art projects)
5. **"We're Going to the Mall!"** — Washington, D.C. (National Mall, federal museums); Houston, Texas (Rothko Chapel, Project Row Houses)
6. **"Embracing and Preserving Community Contribution Through Public Art"** — Philadelphia (Graffiti Pier); Brooklyn, New York (Brooklyn Museum, Brooklyn Public Library)

### Season 2
Slated for release on NOT REAL ART in **July 2025**.

### Description
The series examines **"how public art in the U.S. reflects and shapes our communities and cultural identities,"** exploring installations, sculptures, murals, and community-engaged artworks across major American cities.
